Travel to Brooklyn

directions from major transit hubs

Penn Station → NJ Transit, Newark Airport, and Amtrak
Take either the 2 3 A or C trains heading towards Downtown or Brooklyn.
If you took the 2 or 3 trains, exit at Nevins Street.
If you took the A or C trains, exit at the Hoyt-Schermerhorn Streets Station.
Grand Central Station → Metro-North and Amtrak
Take the 4 or 5 trains heading towards Downtown or Brooklyn.
Exit at Nevins Street.

Nearby Airports

JFK → John F. Kennedy International Airport
Take the AirTrain to Howard Beach Station.
Transfer to the A train heading towards Inwood.
Exit at the Hoyt-Schermerhorn Streets Station.
LGA → LaGuardia Airport
Start by taking the Q70-SBS bus to Roosevelt Av / 74th St.
Transfer to the F train heading towards Coney Island.
Exit at the Jay Street - Metrotech Station.
From here, you can choose to walk or transfer to the C train to Hoyt-Schermerhorn Streets.
EWR → Newark Liberty International Airport
Take the AirTrain to the Newark Airport Station.
Take the Northeast Corridor line heading towards New York Penn Station.
Follow the Penn Station directions above.

Q & A

What should I wear?
The dress code is formal, or black tie optional (emphasis on the optional). New York tends to get chilly in October, so you might want to bring a jacket or wrap.
We also advise against wearing stiletto heels, as they may be unsuitable for walking on the brick floor at the Boathouse.
Do you have a gift registry?
Please know that your presence at our wedding is present enough! However, for friends and family who have been asking for gift ideas, we’ve created an online registry on The Knot.
What precautions will be taken due to COVID-19?
Event guidelines will be based on current NY State and Local NYC Guidance at the time of the event. Currently, all attendees must be fully vaccinated in order to attend the wedding ceremony and reception. If you are unable to meet these requirements, please let us know as soon as possible. For those who will be attending, please bring proof of vaccination.
Additionally, we recommend that anyone who is attending a large scale event in the 1-2 weeks leading up to the wedding receive a negative PCR test result as an additional precaution.

Absolutely not.
