About Us

George and Maddie met in 2012 when both were staying on campus at Carnegie Mellon for the summer. Invited by mutual friends, Maddie attended a barbecue at Kappa Sigma (George's fraternity) on the 4th of July. They met while George was grilling kofta on the front porch. They talked a bit and became friends. Over the following winter, Maddie regularly delivered cookies to George at his house as part of her sorority's fundraiser.

In the spring, they were at a crowded party together and someone needed to cut a lemon to prepare a cocktail. George pulled out a knife to help at the same moment Maddie reached for the lemon, and he accidentally cut her hand. Always prepared, he ran out of the room and immediately came back with a first aid kit. George's heroics did not go unnoticed, and they began dating shortly after.

On March 14th, 2020, George proposed to Maddie in their apartment in the East Village. At the time, they were at the beginning of a "temporary" work-from-home period, which ended up being not so temporary. They spent the next year working from opposite ends of their dining room table, and spending every minute together. Almost exactly a year after their engagement, they moved to Park Slope, where they now split their time between home improvement projects, reading on the balcony, and exploring their new neighborhood. They are both really looking forward to married life.

Q & A

What should I wear?
The dress code is formal, or black tie optional (emphasis on the optional). New York tends to get chilly in October, so you might want to bring a jacket or wrap.
We also advise against wearing stiletto heels, as they may be unsuitable for walking on the brick floor at the Boathouse.
Do you have a gift registry?
Please know that your presence at our wedding is present enough! However, for friends and family who have been asking for gift ideas, we’ve created an online registry on The Knot.
What precautions will be taken due to COVID-19?
Event guidelines will be based on current NY State and Local NYC Guidance at the time of the event. Currently, all attendees must be fully vaccinated in order to attend the wedding ceremony and reception. If you are unable to meet these requirements, please let us know as soon as possible. For those who will be attending, please bring proof of vaccination.
Additionally, we recommend that anyone who is attending a large scale event in the 1-2 weeks leading up to the wedding receive a negative PCR test result as an additional precaution.
